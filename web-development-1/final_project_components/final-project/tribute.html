Tribute


The first Echo, which was a clone of the founder Dr. Joe Oak, was cloned in 2006, a little over a decade before the opening of this technology to the public. At the time, the name of the company was not yet considered, so we dubbed this first clone "Echo", since it was an echo of Dr. Oak. Echo was full of life and ecstatic to be alive, just as excited as Dr. Oak that it was a success. Unfortunately, since this was the first prototype of our technology, Echo had incredibly fragile skin and lacked a properly functioning amygdala, the part of the brain which is responsible for feelings of fear. Only a months after being created, Echo was tragically killed by another newer prototype clone after instigating a fight with it. In memory of our first clone, we named our cloning technology Echo.


Besides just being the first clone, Echo has had an important lasting impact on the direction we have taken. We do extensive checks after a clone is created to ensure they are properly adjusted to their new environment. Though there has never been a clone that has had defects since our technology became public, the checks also involve ensuring that the clone is healthy and ready to live a life.


The pain of losing Echo still haunts our team to this day. There has never been a lack of mourning for Echo, despite the incident happening so long ago. We strive to be as respectful to all fallen clones, as we believe them to be equal to their original person. While it is a tragedy, we know Echo would want us to keep moving forward, and that is exactly what we did and will keep doing.
